Non-Destructive Testing (NDT)

I was trained in and assisted with three primary NDT methods used for inspecting weldments and machined surfaces:

Ultrasonic Testing (UT)

Used for detecting internal flaws (voids, inclusions, cracks) in thick-walled components. A high-frequency sound wave is transmitted through the material and reflections from internal discontinuities are analyzed. I assisted in calibrating the UT equipment, applying couplant, scanning weld zones, and interpreting A-scan displays for accept/reject decisions based on client specifications.

Magnetic Particle Testing (MPT)

Applied to ferromagnetic materials for detecting surface and near-surface cracks. The component is magnetized and fine ferromagnetic particles (dry or wet) are applied. Cracks disrupt the magnetic field lines, causing particles to accumulate and form visible indications. I assisted with demagnetization procedures and documentation of findings.

Dye Penetrant Testing (DPT)

A surface inspection method applicable to non-porous materials. A liquid penetrant is applied, allowed to seep into surface cracks by capillary action, excess is removed, and a developer is applied to draw the penetrant out, revealing crack indications. Used extensively on machined surfaces and weld toes.

Components Inspected

The facility produced a range of heavy-duty components for demanding applications:

  • Drive Shafts — Multi-step shafts with tight tolerances on journals, keyways, and shoulders. Inspected for dimensional accuracy, surface finish, and concentricity.
  • Weldment Assemblies — Large fabricated structures requiring UT and MPT on critical weld joints before machining.
  • Bearing Housings — Internal bore dimensions inspected with bore gauges to ensure proper bearing fit.
  • Flanges & Couplings — Face flatness, bolt circle diameter, and bore concentricity checked against engineering drawings.

Documentation & ISO Compliance

All inspection activities followed the facility's ISO 9001:2015 quality management system. I assisted with:

  • Inspection & Test Plans (ITP) — Master documents listing all QC checkpoints for each component, referencing applicable standards and acceptance criteria.
  • QC Clearance Slips — Release documents confirming that all inspection stages have been passed before dispatch.
  • NDT Reports — Documented findings from UT, MPT, and DPT inspections with accept/reject decisions.
  • Dimensional Reports — Recorded measured values against drawing tolerances, noting any deviations.
  • Material Test Certificates — Verified incoming material certifications against purchase specifications.
