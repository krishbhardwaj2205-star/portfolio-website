PLC-Controlled Universal Testing Machine

IkodeAutomation, Pune December 2025 Automation Intern

Project Overview

During my internship at IkodeAutomation, Pune, I independently designed, assembled, and commissioned a PLC-controlled Universal Testing Machine (UTM) for evaluating paper tear strength. The project involved the complete lifecycle — from mechanical frame assembly to electrical panel wiring, PLC programming, HMI configuration, and sensor integration.

The UTM was built to apply controlled tensile force on paper samples clamped between two jaws. A servo motor drives a ball screw linear actuator, while a load cell measures the applied force in real-time. The PLC orchestrates the entire test cycle — clamping, pulling, measuring, and stopping — with all parameters displayed on a touchscreen HMI.

Mechanical Assembly

The UTM frame was built using 40×40 mm aluminum extrusion profiles, providing a rigid and modular structure. The linear motion system consists of a ball screw driven by a servo motor, mounted with precision-machined brackets. Two linear guide rails ensure smooth, backlash-free vertical motion of the movable crosshead.

A critical engineering challenge was the clamping mechanism. The initial design couldn't hold paper securely during testing. I independently redesigned the jaw geometry and grip surfaces, creating a solution that maintained consistent clamping force without damaging the sample.

Electrical Panel Design

I designed and wired the complete electrical control panel from scratch. The panel includes:

  • Delta DVP-14SS2 PLC — Main controller with digital I/O for controlling the test sequence
  • ASDA-B2 Servo Drive — Controls the servo motor for precise linear motion
  • 24V SMPS — Switched-mode power supply for PLC and sensor power
  • MCBs & Relays — Circuit protection and signal isolation
  • Terminal blocks — Organized wiring for all I/O connections
  • DIN rail mounting — Professional component layout with labeling

All wiring was done with proper cable management, ferrule crimping, and labeling per industrial standards.

PLC Programming & HMI Configuration

The PLC was programmed in Ladder Logic using Delta's WPLSoft/ISPSoft environment. The program handles:

  • Automatic test cycle: clamp → pull at constant speed → detect peak force → stop
  • Manual jog mode for setup and calibration
  • Emergency stop with safe motor deceleration
  • Load cell analog input processing and real-time force display
  • Servo positioning with precise speed and acceleration control

HMI Interface

The Delta DOP-107CV (7-inch color touchscreen) was configured using DOPSoft to provide:

  • Real-time force vs. displacement trend plot
  • Test parameter settings (speed, force limit, sample length)
  • Alarm management and status indicators
  • Test result logging and peak force display

Sensors & Instrumentation

The UTM integrates several sensors for measurement and safety:

  • S-type Load Cell — Measures tensile force applied to the paper sample. Connected via a signal amplifier to the PLC's analog input module.
  • Proximity Sensors — Detect the upper and lower travel limits of the crosshead for safety cutoff.
  • Servo Encoder — Provides precise position and speed feedback for closed-loop motion control.

Challenges & Solutions

Clamping Mechanism Redesign

The original clamping design failed to hold thin paper samples securely during testing. The paper would slip under load, invalidating test results. I analyzed the failure mode and independently designed a new jaw mechanism with serrated grip surfaces and an adjustable clamping force mechanism, which successfully resolved the issue.

Panel Space Optimization

The control enclosure had limited space for all components. I reorganized the DIN rail layout to accommodate the PLC, servo drive, SMPS, relays, and terminal blocks while maintaining proper spacing for heat dissipation and cable routing.

Servo-PLC Communication

Configuring the ASDA-B2 servo drive to communicate properly with the DVP PLC required careful pulse/direction signal setup, acceleration ramp tuning, and electronic gearing ratio calculation.
